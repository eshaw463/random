"Monet demonstrated that no absolute colour exists in Nature, only light; and that since the appearance of all objects changes perpetually, the art of the painter should consist in choosing one moment in preference to any other, in order to fix its individuality in a definitive image." - Dictionary of Modern Painting, pg.196

About the painter: Claude Monet was a French painter who lived from 1840-1926. He was notable for his influence on and role in the impressionism movement along with his contemporaries such as Van Gogh and Ã‰douard Manet. As the quote suggests, his exceptional paintings captured color not as a defined property but as a result of the light in a scene. Coupled with brush strokes melting together seen in impressionism, Monet's portrayal of light captures the beauty of the world.
